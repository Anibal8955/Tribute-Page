While David Ortiz's major league career began in Minnesota, he was best known for his 14 seasons in Boston as the designated hitter for the Red Sox.

David Ortiz graduated from Estudio Espillat High School in the Dominican Republic, where he played baseball and basketball. His career began with the Seattle Mariners organization in 1992. In 1996, he joined the Minnesota Twins, and his major league debut came with the Twins in 1997. After that team released him in 2002, He was signed by the Red Sox in early 2003.

In 2004, he helped the Red Sox end the so-called "Curse of the Bambino" by winning the World Series, the team's first championship since 1918. Ortiz was also on the Red Sox team that won titles in 2007 and 2013. He was named MVP MVP of the series in 2013 when Ortiz, then 37, hit .688 with two home runs, six RBIs and eight walks, finishing with a .760 on-base percentage. In his 20 major league seasons, Ortiz hit 541 home runs and had a .286 batting average. Ortiz retired at the end of the 2016 season, and the Red Sox retired his uniform number in 2017. He will be eligible to be inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame in late 2021 (for the class of 2022).
